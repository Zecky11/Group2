Typhoon Haiyan Relief Efforts and the Coordination of Aid through Social Media Platforms

This website highlights the critical role of social media in mobilizing and coordinating aid during and after Typhoon Haiyan, one of the most devastating storms in history. It explores how digital platforms facilitated real-time communication, emergency response, and global fundraising efforts..

When Typhoon Haiyan struck the Philippines in 2013, millions of people required immediate assistance. The storm, one of the strongest tropical cyclones in recorded history, caused widespread devastation across the country, particularly in areas like Tacloban City.

Spreading Verified Information

Follow official government agencies (e.g., NDRRMC, PAGASA, Philippine Red Cross) for real-time updates. Share only confirmed updates from official sources, using relevant hashtags to increase visibility.

Finding & Assisting Missing Persons

Utilize social media platforms to post and check missing person reports, employing tools like Google Docs to consolidate information and Facebook's safety check-in feature..

Coordinating Donations & Aid
Track legitimate donation drives on social media, share donation links, and contact organizations for volunteer opportunities. Contact organizations via social media direct messages for volunteer opportunities.

Connecting with Rescue Teams

Tag rescue teams, local government, and emergency hotlines when posting urgent calls for help. providing clear location details and updates on areas needing immediate assistance.
